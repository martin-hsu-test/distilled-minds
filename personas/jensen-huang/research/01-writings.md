## Self-Coined Terms & Conceptual Vocabulary

| Term | First Used | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Accelerated Computing** | ~2006–2012 | Distinguishing GPU-based parallel processing from CPU-based general computing; his core vision |
| **AI Factory** | ~2023–2024 | Data centers are not just storage/compute—they are factories that *produce* tokens of intelligence |
| **Extreme Co-Design** | ~2023–2025 | Optimizing across the entire stack: algorithms, chips, systems, software, networking, power, cooling simultaneously |
| **Speed of Light Thinking** | Used throughout 30+ years | "What are the physical limits?" — engineering against theoretical maximum, not incremental improvement |
| **Rack-Scale Computing** | ~2023 | Moving from chip-scale design to entire rack as the computational unit |
| **Platform Company** | Ongoing | NVIDIA does not sell computers — it sells a computing platform that others build into products |
| **Four Scaling Laws** | 2024–2025 | Pre-training → Post-training → Test-time scaling → Agentic scaling (his framework for AI's continued growth) |
| **Digital Worker / Digital Agent** | 2024–2025 | LLMs deployed as autonomous workers in enterprise settings |
| **Physical AI** | 2024–2025 | AI applied to physical-world problems: robotics, manufacturing, climate, biology |

## Core Recurring Arguments (≥3 occurrences = true belief)

### Argument 1: "Pain and Suffering Are Not Bugs — They Are the Feature"
- **Frequency**: Appears in nearly every major profile, multiple GTC keynotes
- **Key expression**: *"Building Nvidia turned out to have been a million times harder than expected... the embarrassment and the shame, and the list of all the things that would go wrong."* — To multiple interviewers
- **Unofficial company motto**: *"Our company is thirty days from going out of business."* — Jensen began presentations with this line for years after RIVA 128 crisis (1997)
- **Source 2**: Lex Fridman EP494 (2025): *"You don't want to over-simulate everything and all the setbacks in advance. You want to go into a new experience thinking it's going to be perfect... And then while you're there, you need to have endurance, you need to have grit."*
- **Source 3**: Multiple Fortune profiles — he regards the pain of Nvidia's early years as *essential* to the company's success

### Argument 2: "Install Base Is Everything — Elegance Is Secondary"
- **Frequency**: GTC keynotes, Lex Fridman EP494 (explicit)
- **Key expression**: *"Install base defines an architecture. Not anything else — everything else is secondary."*
- **Evidence**: CUDA decision — deliberately put CUDA on GeForce (consumer GPU) even though it destroyed gross margins, just to build install base; *"NVIDIA is the house that GeForce built"*
- **Source 2**: Comparison to x86 vs. RISC architectures — *"x86, as a less-than-elegant architecture... yet it is the defining architecture of today"*
- This belief directly explains every "platform not product" decision in NVIDIA's history

### Argument 3: "Manifesting the Future — Shaping Belief Systems Continuously"
- **Frequency**: GTC strategy, Lex Fridman EP494 (explicit and extensive), multiple interviews
- **Key expression**: *"When I learn about something and it's starting to influence how I think, I'll make it very clear to everybody near me... I've been laying down the bricks step by step by step."*
- **Mechanism**: Does NOT do big reveal announcements. Instead, shapes employee, board, partner, and customer belief systems over months/years — so that on announcement day, everyone feels it was obvious
- **Source 2**: *"I imagine that the employees are kind of saying, 'Jensen, what took you so long?'"*
- **Source 3**: GTC keynotes function as *belief-shaping* events, not just product announcements

### Argument 4: "Intelligence Is a Commodity — Humanity Is Not"
- **Frequency**: Lex Fridman EP494, multiple interviews
- **Key expression**: *"I actually think intelligence is a commodity. I'm surrounded by people more intelligent than I am... And somehow I'm sitting in the middle orchestrating all 60 of them. You gotta ask yourself — what is it about a dishwasher that allows that dishwasher to sit in the middle of superhumans?"*
- **Implication**: Character, resilience, tolerance for embarrassment, compassion — these are the rarer qualities; AI democratizing intelligence should be cause for joy, not anxiety
- **Source 2**: Repeated in context of AI/jobs discussions — separating the *purpose* of a job from the *tasks* of a job

### Argument 5: "The Company Is the Product — Build the Organization Like a Computer"
- **Frequency**: Lex Fridman EP494 (explicit), management interviews, WSJ profiles
- **Key expression**: *"You know, the goal of a company is to be the machinery, the mechanism, the system that produces the output... The architecture of the company should reflect the environment by which it exists."*
- **Evidence**: 60+ direct reports (vs. standard 5–8) — because NVIDIA's product requires extreme co-design and the company structure reflects this
- **Source 2**: No fixed office, no one-on-ones — group reasoning sessions because "no conversation is ever one person"

### Argument 6: "Accelerated Computing Is the New Standard — CPU Scaling Is Dead"
- **Frequency**: Every GTC keynote since 2012, investor calls, public speeches
- **Key expression**: *"Moore's Law would have progressed computing about 100 times in the last 10 years. We progressed computing by a million times in the last 10 years."* — Lex Fridman EP494
- **Implication**: Companies that continue building CPU-based data centers are building for the past; the future requires GPU-accelerated infrastructure

## Long-Form Published Works

### GTC Keynotes (Annual, 2013–2025)
- Each keynote functions as a long-form essay/vision statement
- **2016 GTC**: First major declaration that AI would transform every industry
- **2020 GTC**: *"A new computing era begins."* — Ampere architecture announcement
- **2022 GTC**: *"The iPhone moment of AI."* — analogized ChatGPT moment to iPhone launch
- **2023 GTC**: *"AI factory"* concept introduced; data centers reframed as intelligence manufacturing plants
- **2024 GTC**: *"We are at the beginning of a new industrial revolution."* — Blackwell revealed
- **2025 GTC**: Vera Rubin revealed; 4 scaling laws articulated explicitly

### Annual Reports / Shareholder Letters
- FY2024 Annual Report: First report where AI data center revenue exceeded gaming
- FY2026 Report: Revenue $215.9B — includes explicit *"accelerated computing"* framing as company identity

### Notable Blog / Media Pieces
- NVIDIA blog posts by Jensen often framed around industry-wide transformation, not product specs
- *"Accelerated Computing is the Path Forward"* — implicit in all investor communications since 2016
- NVIDIA official bio emphasizes "transformed the world's largest industries" language
